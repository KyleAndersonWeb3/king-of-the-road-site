The $940 Billion Industry Nobody Talks About

Let me paint you a picture.

A 22-year-old is grinding at 2 AM, chasing a $75K entry-level job while rent eats half his paycheck. Meanwhile, a 28-year-old owner-operator in Texas grossed $320,000 last year hauling flatbed freight. No student debt. Owns his truck outright. Shopping for his second.

No TikTok told him to do this. No pitch deck. Just a guy, a truck, and an industry that moves $940 billion worth of freight every single year.

That's trucking. And most people have no idea.

Here's what most people don't know: the American trucking industry generates $940.8 billion in gross freight revenue annually. That's bigger than Saudi Arabia's entire GDP. Trucking moves 72.6% of all domestic freight — 11.46 billion tons every year.

Everything you touched today — your coffee, your phone, your food — got there on a truck. There's no American economy without trucking. And while everyone's fighting over the same overhyped industries? Trucking just sits here. Massive. Essential. Genuinely desperate for people.

The driver shortage is your opportunity. The industry is short approximately 60,000 to 82,000 drivers right now. That number is projected to hit 160,000+ by 2030. The average truck driver is 49 years old. Boomers and Gen X are retiring faster than new drivers can replace them.

What does a massive, worsening labor shortage mean for you? Leverage. Opportunity. Show up competent and reliable — and this industry will pay you well for decades.

Real income numbers:

Company Drivers: $65,000–$85,000/year
Owner-Operators (net): $70,000–$150,000+/year
Fleet Owners (5 trucks): $150,000–$300,000+/year
